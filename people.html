We are teachers, parents, grandparents, business leaders, and members of the Plymouth-Canton community who are dedicated to excellence through innovation in public education.






Cyndi Burnstein


School Leader

Cyndi taught English at Salem High School where she served as a leader in innovative education for 40 years. At Plymouth Canton Educational Park, she designed the Humanities course and co-founded the award-winning Spectra program, an interdisciplinary school-within-a school that dramatically reduced failure rates in 9th and 10th grade core classes. Cyndi brings considerable knowledge and experience in leading organizational change at the high school level. She authored the $1.2 million US Dept. of Education Smaller Learning Communities grant and served as its coordinator, building capacity for teacher collaboration. She earned her M.A. in Educational Leadership from Eastern Michigan University and has published numerous articles on projects-based learning, in addition to presenting at local, state, and national conferences. Cyndi is the recipient of the PCEA Sharon Belobradich Leadership Award, MEA Showcasing Success Award, Wayne County RESA Golden Apple, NCTE Center of Excellence Award, and three Plymouth-Canton Community Schools Mary Beth Carroll Extra Miler awards. For 37 years she and her husband Jim have been residents of Plymouth, Michigan where they raised their three children, Gabe, Devin, and Jake.

Carey Gary


Board of Education

Carey is a successful small business owner and exemplary educator, with over 40 years experience in secondary and postsecondary vocational education. She co-authored the State of Michigan curriculum on Hospitality Education, developed a culinary arts competition for high school students on both state and national levels, and has pioneered outcome-based vocational learning for students – specifically those at risk. Carey brings a wealth of experience to the New School High team in projects-based curriculum development and teacher mentorship, in addition to her acumen as an entrepreneur in the Plymouth/Canton community. Carey and her husband Jon, a former culinary arts teacher, have been residents of Plymouth for over 20 years where they raised their two daughters.

Gayla Houser


Board of Education

As a successful business leader in southeastern Michigan, Gayla brings vast experience in marketing, communications, and leadership to New School High. Gayla is currently the Director of Business Development at the law firm of Miller Canfield, and has previously served as President and CEO of the Troy Chamber of Commerce and as Vice-President of Detroit’s Central Business District Association. She has strived to connect business professionals to local schools by hosting annual briefings with high school guidance counselors and human resource managers, supporting community-wide job fairs, and establishing family care services for employees. Gayla and her husband are residents of Troy, Michigan, where they raised their two children, Ross and Anna.

Joanne Lamar


Board of Education

Joanne brings a unique, wellness-focused perspective to New School High as a registered nurse who regularly practices in urban areas throughout southeastern Michigan. Having recently completed her M.A. in Public Policy, Joanne hopes to apply her passion and expertise in healthcare to education, developing a health-conscious community for students, teachers, and families. Joanne is a former member of the Plymouth-Canton Community Schools Board of Education. She is also an active supporter of the arts, as well as former president of the Plymouth-Canton Marching Band Boosters. Joanne and her husband have lived in Plymouth for over 30 years where they raised their two children and now enjoy spending time with their four grandchildren.

Karen Phillippi


Board of Education

A proud graduate of Michigan State University, Karen led the immigration law team at Miller Canfield for eight years before becoming Deputy Director at the Office for New Americans for the State of Michigan in 2014. Karen also serves as the President of Global Detroit, a non-profit organization that utilizes the experience of immigration as a means for economic growth in the city. Her extensive knowledge of law and her global perspective add a vital dimension to the New School High team. Karen was raised by two educators in Plymouth, Michigan and attended Salem High School, where she was a student in Cyndi Burnstein’s Humanities course. Karen moved back to Plymouth in 2000 where she lives with her son Tyler.

Mike Wright


Board of Education

Mike and his wife Nancy are 28-year residents of Plymouth, where they raised their two sons. As a current City Council member, Mike brings considerable experience to the New School High team in local governance and administration. In the late 1980’s he co-founded the Generation of Promise, a diversity and leadership training program for juniors in sixteen Metro Detroit high schools. He currently serves as its Board Chairman. Mike attributes his passion for service to his parents, both dedicated public servants who lived and worked in the City of Detroit. Beyond his civic involvement, Mike worked as the Executive Vice President of the Detroit Metropolitan Convention and Visitors Bureau before becoming a small business owner in 2000.
